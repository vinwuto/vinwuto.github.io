Renovation Benefits


Retrofitting buildings to be more green can lead to significant cost savings, environmental benefits, improved tenant comfort and health, increased property value, and government incentives:

Reduction in Usage

The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency estimates that green building practices can reduce energy use by up to 50%, carbon dioxide emissions by up to 39%, water use by up to 30%, and waste generation by up to 70%.

Comfort Improvements

Retrofitting a building with green infrastructure, such as green roofs, living walls, and rain gardens, can help to reduce the urban heat island effect, improve air quality, and reduce stormwater runoff.

Sustainable Materials


Sustainable materials are those that are produced using environmentally friendly processes, and are often made from renewable resources or recycled materials.

Bamboo

Bamboo is a fast-growing, renewable material that can be used for flooring, furniture, and other home products. It is also highly durable and resistant to moisture, making it ideal for use in humid climates.

Cork

Cork is a sustainable material made from the bark of the cork oak tree. It is often used as a flooring material because of its natural cushioning and insulation properties. Cork is also water-resistant and easy to clean.

Reclaimed Wood

Using reclaimed wood for furniture, flooring, and other home products is a sustainable option because it reduces the demand for new timber. Reclaimed wood can come from old buildings, barns, and other structures that are being demolished.

Recycled Plastic

Recycled plastic can be used to create a variety of home products, including furniture, decking, and outdoor decor. Using recycled plastic helps to reduce the amount of plastic waste in the environment and conserves natural resources.

Wool

Wool is a renewable and biodegradable material that can be used for rugs, blankets, and other textiles. It is naturally flame-retardant and hypoallergenic, making it a safe and eco-friendly option for home products.

Native Species


Native species are important to consider because they have adapted over millions of years to fit the surrounding environment. They seamlessly interact with other native flora and provide important resources to other native species.

Green Infrastructure


Green infrastructure refers to natural systems and elements, such as trees, green roofs, and wetlands, that can be used to manage stormwater, reduce urban heat islands, and improve air quality. Incorporating green infrastructure into urban development can provide a range of environmental, social, and economic benefits, including reducing flooding, improving public health, and enhancing property values.

Green Roofs

Green roofs involve planting vegetation on the roof of a building, which provides numerous benefits such as reducing stormwater runoff, improving air quality, and reducing the urban heat island effect.

Green roofs can be extensive (with a shallow soil layer and low-maintenance plants) or intensive (with a deeper soil layer and more complex plants).

Green Walls

Green walls (also known as living walls or vertical gardens) involve growing plants on a vertical surface, such as a wall.

Green walls can be used to improve air quality, reduce noise pollution, and provide aesthetic benefits.

Read More

Rain Gardens

Rain gardens are shallow depressions planted with native vegetation that can capture and filter stormwater runoff. They can help reduce the amount of pollutants that enter waterways and recharge groundwater supplies. According to the EPA, rain gardens can reduce stormwater runoff by up to 30%.

Energy Efficiency


By optimizing building systems and upgrading equipment, buildings can save significant amounts of energy and money while improving comfort and health for occupants. Improving energy efficiency can also improve the value and marketability of a building, as more and more buyers and tenants are looking for sustainable and energy-efficient properties.

Energy-efficient Buildings

The concept of energy-efficient buildings focuses on reducing the amount of energy used in buildings without compromising the quality of the indoor environment.

Energy-efficient buildings typically have features such as insulation, high-performance windows, efficient heating and cooling systems, and energy-efficient lighting.

Renewable Energy

Renewable energy refers to energy sources that are replenished naturally, such as solar, wind, and hydro power.

Renewable energy systems can be installed on buildings to provide on-site power generation and reduce reliance on fossil fuels.

Waste Reduction and Repurposing


Repurposing waste is a critical strategy for creating a more sustainable and environmentally conscious society. Repurposing waste involves finding new and creative uses for it, reducing the amount of waste that ultimately ends up in landfills. Repurposing waste can be a cost-effective way for businesses and communities to generate revenue, create jobs, and support local economies.

Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting involves collecting and storing rainwater for later use, such as for irrigation or flushing toilets.

Rainwater harvesting systems typically include a catchment area (such as a roof), gutters and downspouts, a storage tank, and a pump.
